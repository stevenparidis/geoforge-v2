Description: Physical Geology textbook chapter on intrusive igneous bodies. Figure 3.5.2 is the key teaching diagram showing all major intrusion types in a single schematic cross-section with labels — dykes cutting across layering, sills parallel to layering, laccoliths doming overlying rock, stocks and batholiths as large equidimensional bodies.

Useful for auditing: Figure 3.5.2 is the primary reference for GeoForge's intrusion class. It establishes the correct geometric relationship between each intrusion type and the surrounding host-rock layering: dykes are discordant (cross-cut), sills are concordant (parallel), laccoliths dome the roof, and batholiths are massive irregular bodies.
